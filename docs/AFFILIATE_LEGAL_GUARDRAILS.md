# Affiliate Legal Guardrails

This document is implementation guidance, not legal advice. Public launch should go through counsel.

## Approved Framing

Use:

- Affiliate
- Referral partner
- Creator ambassador
- Fan ambassador
- Multi-tier affiliate program
- Referral network
- Commission on qualified purchases
- Pending commission
- Approved commission
- Paid commission
- Wallet balance
- Payout request

Avoid:

- Investment
- Profit guarantee
- Passive income guarantee
- Pyramid
- Recruiting income
- Downline profits
- Staking rewards
- ROI
- Financial freedom

## Commissionable Events

Commissions may be created only from qualified purchases or approved transactions:

- Product purchase
- Membership purchase
- Creator shop purchase
- Campaign merchandise purchase
- Digital product purchase
- Approved subscription purchase
- Approved commercial transaction

Do not commission affiliate signup alone, recruitment alone, mandatory starter kits, pay-to-join fees, or inventory loading without legal review.

## 7-Level Language

Use "7-level ambassador commission system", "multi-tier affiliate program", "referral network", and "qualified purchase commission".

Avoid "MLM", "downline", "recruiting income", "passive income guarantee", "business opportunity", "ROI", and "financial freedom" in customer-facing copy.

The system may support levels 0 through 7 only when every paid commission is tied to a qualified product, membership, campaign, digital goods, creator-shop, or approved commercial transaction.

## Disclosure Requirements

Affiliates, creators, and fan ambassadors must disclose when they may receive a commission or store credit. Disclosures should be clear, easy to notice, and close to the link, coupon, product card, post, video, or livestream recommendation.

## Reversal And Review

FootprintsHub may reject, reverse, cap, or hold commissions for:

- Self-referrals
- Duplicate or suspicious activity
- Refunds
- Chargebacks
- Excluded products or categories
- Missing disclosure
- Policy violations
- Manual compliance review

## FTC Reference

FTC guidance says earnings claims should be truthful, substantiated, and non-misleading. It also warns against misleading representations about typical earnings, expenses, lifestyle results, or hypothetical income scenarios.

Source: https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/business-guidance-concerning-multi-level-marketing
